Doctor's Tips

Suggestions on what to log and track for your chronic headache diary.

📋 Why These Details Matter

  • Patterns across episodes help your doctor identify headache type (migraine, tension, cluster, etc.)
  • Tracking triggers helps you and your doctor prevent future episodes
  • Medication logs show what's working and prevent overuse headache

🍽 Food Triggers to Watch

  • Log everything you ate 24 hours before a headache starts
  • Common triggers: aged cheese, chocolate, alcohol (especially red wine), processed meats, MSG
  • Skipping meals or dehydration are very common triggers — log water intake
  • Caffeine: both too much and withdrawal can cause headaches

😴 Sleep & Lifestyle

  • Note your sleep hours — both too little and too much can trigger headaches
  • Track your stress level (even just low/medium/high)
  • Note any screen time before headache onset
  • Physical activity, posture, and eye strain are also common factors

💊 Medication Tracking

  • Always note the exact name and dose of medication taken
  • Using pain relievers more than 2–3x/week can cause "rebound" (medication overuse) headaches
  • Note how long after taking medication you felt relief (or didn't)
  • Bring this diary to every doctor visit

🔴 When to Seek Immediate Help

  • "Thunderclap" headache — sudden, worst headache of your life
  • Headache with fever, stiff neck, confusion, or rash
  • Headache after head injury
  • New headache after age 50
  • Headache with vision, speech, or limb problems

📤 Sharing With Your Doctor

  • Use the Export button on your Home tab to download your log as a text summary
  • Bring to every consult — even photos of this diary app screen help
  • Mention how headaches affect your daily activities (work, sleep, chores)
